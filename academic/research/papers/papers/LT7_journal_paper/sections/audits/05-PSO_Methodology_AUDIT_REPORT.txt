## 1. Executive Summary

Section 5 presents the PSO methodology for optimizing controller gains. The structure is comprehensive, covering algorithm details, fitness function design, and multi-scenario validation. However, the audit identified several **SEVERITY 2 (HIGH)** issues related to numerical inconsistencies in reported improvement ratios and potential "magic number" discrepancies in cost weights.

Crucially, the **$\beta \neq 1$ issue** from Section 4 reappears in the parameter bounds discussion (Section 5.3), where theoretical conditions are checked against bounds without explicitly accounting for the effective gain scaling. This reinforces the need for a global fix on the $\beta$ assumption.

## 2. Detailed Findings

### 2.1 Mathematical Rigor & Implicit Assumptions

*   **Implicit $\beta=1$ in Parameter Bounds (SEVERITY 1):**
    *   **Location:** Section 5.3, "STA SMC" bounds justification.
    *   **Text:** "For DIP system with $\bar{d} \approx 0.2$, $\beta \approx 1.0$ (from Section 2)..."
    *   **Fact:** Section 2 does *not* state $\beta=1.0$. Section 4 Example 4.1 calculates $\beta \approx 0.78$.
    *   **Impact:** The justification $K_1 > 0.6$ relies on $\beta=1$. If $\beta=0.78$, the lower bound requirement increases: $K_1 > \frac{2\sqrt{2(0.2)}}{\sqrt{0.78}} \approx \frac{1.26}{0.88} \approx 1.43$. The current lower bound of $2.0$ is still safe ($2.0 > 1.43$), but the *reasoning* is mathematically incorrect.
    *   **Fix:** Correct the text to "$\beta \approx 0.78$" and re-calculate the theoretical lower bound.

*   **Dimensional Analysis of Cost Function:**
    *   **Equation:** $J(\mathbf{g}) = \dots + w_{rate} \Delta U_{norm} + \dots$
    *   **Check:** Weights are dimensionless multipliers for normalized terms. This is consistent.
    *   **Normalization:** $ISE_{norm} = ISE/ISE_0$. If $ISE_0$ has same units as $ISE$, term is dimensionless.
    *   **Status:** Valid.

### 2.3 Cross-Section Consistency

*   **Degradation Claim Mismatch (SEVERITY 2):**
    *   **Abstract:** "50.4x chattering degradation".
    *   **Section 5.5:** "144.59x chattering degradation".
    *   **Section 8.3:** "49.28x" (calculated from previous audit).
    *   **Conflict:** There are three different numbers for the "degradation ratio" (50.4x vs 144.6x vs 49.3x). This is a major inconsistency. Likely due to different datasets (training vs validation) or metrics (Nominal vs Realistic).
    *   **Hypothesis:** Section 5.5 uses "Robust PSO" validation data (2000 simulations), while Abstract/Section 8 use the "Generalization Limitations" dataset (400 simulations).
    *   **Fix:** Harmonize these numbers. Pick one definitive dataset for the "degradation" claim and use it consistently.

*   **Parameter Bounds:**
    *   **Section 3.9:** Recommends $K \in [1.2, 50]$.
    *   **Section 5.3:** Sets bounds $K \in [0.2, 5.0]$ for Classical SMC.
    *   **Conflict:** The upper bound in PSO (5.0) is much lower than the manual tuning upper limit (50.0). This might artificially constrain the PSO if optimal gains lie above 5.0 (e.g. for large disturbances).
    *   **Fix:** Justify the tighter bound in 5.3 or widen it.
