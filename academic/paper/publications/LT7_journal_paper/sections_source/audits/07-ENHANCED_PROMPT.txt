## 1. Executive Summary

Section 7 presents the core performance results of the study. The presentation is clear, with well-structured tables and detailed statistical analysis. The use of effect sizes (Cohen's d) and confidence intervals enhances the rigor of the findings.

However, the audit identified **SEVERITY 2 (HIGH)** issues regarding the "Degradation Ratio" calculation (again) and potential unit confusion in chattering metrics. ## 2. Detailed Findings

### 2.1 Mathematical Rigor & Implicit Assumptions

*   **Chattering Metric Consistency (SEVERITY 2):**
    *   **Table 7.3:** "Chattering Index" values are 8.2, 2.1, 9.7, 5.4.
    *   **Section 6.2:** Defines CI as RMS derivative [N/s].
    *   **Section 5.5 Table:** Reports "Nominal Chattering" as 797.34.
    *   **Conflict:** Section 5.5 likely reports the *sum of squared derivatives* (unnormalized), while Section 7 reports the *RMS* (normalized).
    *   **Calculation Check:** If $CI_{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{1}{N} \sum (\dot{u})^2} \approx 2.1$. Then $\sum (\dot{u})^2 \approx 2.1^2 \times 1000 = 4.41 \times 1000 = 4410$. This does not match 797.34. The units or normalization factors are inconsistent between sections.

*   **Theoretical Predictions (SEVERITY 3):**
    *   **Table 7.9:** "Classical SMC Predicted Settling: 2.0-2.2s".
    *   **Equation 4.5:** Prediction depends on $\lambda$. If $\lambda \approx 10$ (Section 3.2), time constant $\tau = 1/\lambda = 0.1s$. 4 time constants = 0.4s.
    *   **Discrepancy:** Why is the prediction 2.0-2.2s? This implies the dominant pole is much slower than $\lambda$. Likely dominated by the sliding mode reaching phase, not the sliding phase. The text references Eq 4.5 (asymptotic), but that only applies *on* the surface.
    *   **Missing Link:** The prediction needs to account for the reaching time $t_{reach} = |s(0)|/K_{eff}$. With $K=15$ and $\beta=0.78$, $K_{eff} \approx 11.7$.

1. MATHEMATICAL RIGOR (For equations, proofs, derivations):

   a) List ALL implicit assumptions
      - Don't assume β=1, d=0, or any parameter = nominal value
      - Check if "obviously" canceling terms actually cancel
      - Verify algebra holds for general case, not just examples

   b) Dimensional analysis
      - Check units on both sides of EVERY equation
      - Flag any dimensionally inconsistent terms

   c) Numerical verification
      - Plug example values into theoretical inequalities
      - Verify claimed bounds are satisfied by numerical data
      - Check if examples are representative or cherry-picked

   d) Edge case analysis
      - What if parameters → 0? What if parameters → ∞?
      - What if β≠1? What if disturbances ≠ 0?
      - Are inequalities valid at stated domain boundaries?

2. DATA/RESULTS RIGOR (For numerical claims, statistics, tables):

   a) Trace EVERY numerical claim to source
      - "50.4x degradation" → Show exact calculation
      - "90.2% failure rate" → Verify from raw data
      - "1.82s settling time" → Confirm appears in tables

   b) Statistical validity
      - Sample size sufficient for claimed CI?
      - p-values corrected for multiple comparisons?
      - Effect sizes match "significant" claims?
      - Test assumptions satisfied (normality, etc.)?

   c) Cross-check values
      - Compare numbers in text vs. tables vs. figures
      - Verify percentages sum to 100% where applicable
      - Check mean ± CI makes sense (positive quantities)

   d) Consistency with theory
      - Do experimental results match theoretical predictions?
      - Are controller rankings consistent with Section 4 proofs?
      - Do numerical values satisfy derived bounds?
